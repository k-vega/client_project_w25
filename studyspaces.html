Navigating the University of Michigan can feel overwhelming at times, but finding the right study space doesn’t have to be. Whether you're preparing for exams, working on group projects, or just need a quiet corner to focus, this guide is here to help you discover the best study spots on campus tailored to your needs. As a first-gen student, you may have different priorities when it comes to study environments. Some may prefer quieter spots with fewer distractions, while others thrive in more social settings. No matter your preference, Michigan’s campus offers a wide range of spaces to suit all study styles.

Mason Hall

Mason Hall is one of the many places on campus where you can study. With access to Angell Hall, it provides versatile environments to suit your study needs.

Top Spots:

  • Fishbowl
  • Angell Hall Living Room
  • Mason Hall Classrooms

Michigan Union

The Michigan Union holds a variety of different spaces to suit your needs. Here you are more likely to find group study, providing a great space for socialization.

Top Spots:

  • Idea Hub
  • Union Basement
  • Living Room Areas

Michigan Union

Next door to the The Michigan Union, the LSA Building provides smaller spaces to help with individual study. If you are an LSA student, you can get access to numerous resources here.

Top Spots:

  • Reserved Study Rooms
  • Open Area
  • Booths

Shapiro Library

Known as one of the busiest libraries on campus, Shapiro has a variety of study spaces, from quiet areas for solo studying to group rooms for collaborative work. If you’re looking for a flexible environment, the Shapiro Library offers a mix of resources and settings to help you get your best work done.

Top Spots:

  • First Floor
  • Third Floor
  • Fourth Floor

Ross School of Business Winter Garden

With a more modern look and feel, the Ross Winter Garden provides the perfect spot to enjoy and aesthetically pleasing environment.
